## Definition
- Let $(x_n)_{n\in\mathbb N}: \mathbb N \to\mathbb R$ be a real sequence
	- $+\infty$ is said to be a limit of the sequence ($\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty} = +\infty$), if $\forall v \in\mathbb R\;\exists m\in\mathbb N\;\forall n\in\mathbb N: x_n > v$ 
	- $-\infty$ is said to be a limit of the sequence ($\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty} = -\infty$), if $\forall v \in\mathbb R\;\exists m\in\mathbb N\;\forall n\in\mathbb N: x_n < v$
