$$\Large M = 35$$
## Task
Write equation for the tangent and normal lines to the curve $\displaystyle\frac{x^2}{M}+(-1)^My^2=1$ at point $(x_1,y_1)$ 
Your solutions should be self-explanatory and proof-carrying!
## Solution
Let us [[Derivative|differentiate]] both parts with respect to $x$
$$\left(\frac{x^2}{35}+(-1)^{35}y^2\right)'=(1)'$$
$$\left(\frac{x^2}{35}-y^2\right)'=(1)'$$
$$\frac{2x}{35}-2yy'=0$$
$\displaystyle y'=\frac{x}{35y}$
Let us substitute $x=x_1, y=y_1:$
$\displaystyle y'=\frac{x_1}{35y_1}$
Tangent line:
$$y=y'(x-x_1)+y_1\implies y=\frac{x_1(x-x_1)}{35y_1}+y_1$$
Normal line:
$$y=\frac{-1}{y'}(x-x_1)+y_1\implies y=\frac{35y_1(x_1-x)}{x_1}+y_1$$
